# SAT-002 — Glasshouse Accord

Status: validated / kit-reviewed / preserved
Disposable/preserved root: `experiments/glasshouse-accord/`
Selected: 2026-07-19

## Immutable Prompt

Mediate a blackout council by hearing three envoys, correcting distorted testimony, binding each verified pledge, and rallying the chamber when accumulated shocks break its resolve.

## Semantic Boundary

Primary stress edge: deterministic rumor distortion/correction feeding negotiation obligation outcomes while collective resolve breaks and rallies.

The route owns authored testimony, visible ordering, player choices, ending rules, browser input, and presentation. It must not own parallel exposure lineage, correction, obligation, settlement, shock, resolve, or rally state.

## Player Loop

```txt
select envoy -> hear testimony -> inspect distortion -> correct the record
-> rally if the chamber breaks -> bind and fulfill verified pledge
-> repeat for three envoys -> unanimous accord or breached ending -> restart
```

Carried state: exposure correction, obligation settlement, accumulated shocks, chamber status, and completed pledges.

## Quality Bar

- A first-time player can identify the accord goal and four hero actions within five seconds.
- Testimony distortion/correction, pledge status, chamber resolve, broken/rallied feedback, and the current envoy are legible without opening diagnostics.
- A premature pledge creates a clear breached-accord loss; verified pledges plus one required rally reach a clear success ending.
- The visual language is a chamber/council composition, not a map, rescue board, or infrastructure scene.
- Advanced domain evidence remains closed by default; restart is deterministic.

## Lessons

- Showing the distorted sentence and corrected sentence makes rumor state playable without dialogue generation.
- A domain-owned breach creates a stronger failure than a generic timer because the player can name the bad decision.
- The break/rally threshold turns accumulated social shocks into a readable midpoint transformation using existing resolve semantics.
- The next unit must avoid social testimony, promises, group morale, rescue, maps, and countdown/infrastructure pressure.
